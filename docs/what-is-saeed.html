What it is

An engineering department, in one command.

You hand SAEED a goal with /saeed:hire. It plans the architecture, splits the work across the right specialists, writes the code, reviews it, tests it, hardens security, and documents everything — then audits its own work and keeps improving until there's nothing worthwhile left to do, or you tell it to stop.

It even upgrades itself: moving to stronger AI models as they ship, and adding or retiring its own specialists. There's a micro-managing “Boss” agent whose whole job is to make sure every other agent actually did the work — to standard, with evidence.

The name: SAEED is the author's name (سعيد, “happy”) and also echoes ساعِد — Arabic for helper. A team named after him, built to help you.

How the work flows

From brief to done — then better, on its own.

Specify

The brief becomes user stories with testable acceptance criteria.

Design

Architecture, decisions, and interface contracts are set.

Build

Specialists implement; work is routed to the right expert.

Gate

Every change passes review + tests. The Boss signs off on evidence.

Improve

The team audits its own work and fixes the highest-value things.

↻ Improve repeats every cycle until the team declares the project converged (nothing worthwhile left) — or you run /saeed:stop.
